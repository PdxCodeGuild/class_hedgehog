About PEP


Introduction

The Pilot's Examiner Planner (PEP) app is a one-stop-shop for a pilot that is seeking a checkride from an FAA Designated Pilot Examiner (DPE). As a pilot trains and moves through the ranks, honing in their skills while earning new ratings and certificates, they must navigate the difficult process of locating and scheduling a DPE to start the initial process when applying for an FAA checkride. This process currently proves to be very cumbersome and often takes an unneccesary amount of time to receive a response regarding scheduling, as the only communication is via email and/or phone, which often leads to unreturned voicemails and/or messages.


The Why


Throughout my aviation journey, I've always noticed that, when trying to schedule a checkride or even just getting in touch with a DPE to do so, has really been a struggle. Usually, once your flight instructor deems that you are checkride-ready, they're the ones that try to schedule your checkride for you. That being said, flight instructors are, often, very busy. They have multiple students that are in different phases of their training and most are seeking different licenses (PPL, instrument, commercial, comm/multi, etc.). You can probably tell how that might become overwhelming for one person to be responsible for. Then there's also the difficulty when it comes to trying to find a DPE that is available within the near future, and that's if your flight instructor can even get ahold of them. With the PEP app, a lot of that stress can be taken off of the flight instructor and the responisbility of scheduling can be placed with the applicant pilot.

Pilot's Examiner Planner aims to make this process much more ergonomic as a 'one-stop-shop,' user-friendly interface that allows users (pilot applicants) to locate examiners based on specific criteria chosen by the user (ie. location, examiner/designee type, authorizations, name, phone, email, etc.). Examiners will also be able to integrate their availability via calendar functionality to allow the user to see when the examiner will be availble next. Both, the user and examiner, will be able to communicate internally, amongst one another, to eliminate the need for pesky phone calls and emails; we'll keep those as a last resort. Pilots will also be able to post, on a blog-type page, where they are located, who they used as their DPE, pass/fail, general expectation of requirements specific to the DPE that they used, and the cost of that DPE (prices range anywhere from $500-$1500 on average and depending on what type of checkride you are doing (instrcutor ratings are generally more expensive and take 6-8 hours on average)).
